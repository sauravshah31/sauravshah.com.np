# Everest Base Camp Trek

The Everest Base Camp trek is one of the most iconic journeys in the world. Standing at the foot of the world's highest mountain, surrounded by towering peaks and glaciers, is an experience that words can barely capture.

## Day 1: Kathmandu to Lukla

The adventure begins with a thrilling 35-minute flight from Kathmandu to Lukla (2,860m). The Tenzing-Hillary Airport is famous for its short runway perched on a mountain slope — one of the most dramatic airport approaches in the world.

> "The mountains are calling and I must go." — John Muir

From Lukla, we trekked to Phakding (2,610m), a gentle 3-hour walk through pine forests and across suspension bridges over the Dudh Koshi river.

## Day 2: Phakding to Namche Bazaar

Today's trek to Namche Bazaar (3,440m) is one of the most beautiful sections of the entire route. The trail crosses several suspension bridges, each one higher than the last, with prayer flags fluttering in the mountain breeze.

## Day 3: Acclimatization at Namche Bazaar

Namche Bazaar is the gateway to the Khumbu region and the last major town before Everest. We spent a rest day here to acclimatize to the altitude.

The town is built in a natural amphitheater, with colorful buildings stacked up the hillside. The Saturday market is a fascinating mix of local Sherpa traders and trekkers from around the world.

## Day 4: Namche to Tengboche

The trail from Namche to Tengboche (3,867m) offers the first clear views of Everest, Lhotse, and Ama Dablam. The famous Tengboche Monastery, the largest in the Khumbu region, sits in a stunning location with Ama Dablam as its backdrop.

## Day 5-7: Tengboche to Dingboche to Lobuche

The landscape becomes increasingly dramatic as we gain altitude. The vegetation thins out, replaced by rocky moraines and glacial lakes. Yaks become the primary mode of transport.

At Lobuche (4,940m), the nights are bitterly cold and the air noticeably thin. Headaches and breathlessness are common companions.

## Day 8: Lobuche to Gorak Shep to EBC

The final push to Everest Base Camp (5,364m) is both physically demanding and emotionally overwhelming. The trail crosses the Khumbu Glacier, a chaotic jumble of ice and rock.

Reaching Base Camp is surreal. There's no view of the summit from here — Everest hides behind the Khumbu Icefall — but the sheer scale of the place is humbling.

**Elevation:** 5,364m  
**Distance from Lukla:** ~65km  
**Time:** 8 days up, 5 days down

## Tips for Future Trekkers

1. **Acclimatize properly** — don't rush the ascent
2. **Hire a local guide** — they know the mountain and support the local economy
3. **Pack light** — porters are available but less is more
4. **Bring cash** — ATMs are only available in Namche
5. **Respect the environment** — carry out all your waste
